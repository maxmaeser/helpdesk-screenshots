A brand represents a single franchise concept within your organization. Each brand operates independently with its own data, workflows, and configuration, so you can manage multiple franchise concepts without any overlap between them.

### What each brand includes

What's included depends on your organization's plan.

Every brand, on any plan, has its own:

- **Sales pipeline**: track and manage franchise applicants through your sales process.
- **Applicant portal**: a dedicated portal where prospective franchisees learn about the brand, submit applications, and interact with your team.
- **Library**: store brand-specific documents and files.
- **Settings**: brand-level configuration including identity (logo, colors, description), domain control, connections, team permissions, scheduling events, and webhooks.

Organizations on the Full plan also get:

- **Marketing audiences**: build and segment marketing lists, run automated marketing workflows.
- **Operations**: manage franchisee audiences, locations, vendors, catalog items, manuals, and courses.

Organizations on the Lite plan are focused on franchise development. They don't include Marketing audiences or Operations, since those modules manage franchisees after they've already opened.

If you're not sure which plan your organization is on, ask your organization admin.

### How brands relate to organizations

A brand sits within an organization. The organization is the parent container that handles billing and team management, while each brand manages its own day-to-day franchise operations. An organization can hold multiple brands, and each brand's data stays isolated from the others.

### Switching between brands

If you have access to more than one brand, you can switch between them from the dashboard. Your view, data, and settings update to reflect the selected brand. The brands you see depend on the permissions your organization admin has granted you.

### Brand setup

When you add a new brand (either during onboarding or later), you go through a brand identity setup process where you configure:

- Brand name and description
- Logo, banner, and favicon
- Color palette
- Social media links (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, X, YouTube)
- Contact information and address

These details appear across the applicant portal and in communications sent on behalf of the brand.

### Where to find brand settings

You can manage brand configuration under **Brand** in your settings. This includes the brand profile, domain control, appearance, scheduling events, team access, and connections (formerly called integrations), plus associated organizations and webhooks.
