Brand is your identity reference. It's a read only space where your brand publishes the standards you operate against. The page opens with the brand mark, the colors, and a short line on how the brand shows up in the market.

## The three standards sections

- **Visual standards**: the page you'll come back to most. Logo lockups, color values, typography, and photography guidance in one reference. Use it anytime you're producing something that carries the brand.
- **Voice and tone**: how the brand speaks. Example phrases show the register your brand uses, with side by side examples of on brand and off brand language so the call is clear.
- **Customer experience**: the moments that matter at your location. Greeting, order taking, handoff, and follow up, each with the standard your brand expects, written out so your team can train to it.

## Requesting an approval

The standards are read only for a reason: they keep the brand consistent across locations. If you have a use case that falls outside the standard, like a local sponsorship, a co marketing piece, or a seasonal variant, click **Request approval** at the top of the page.

The form asks for the use case, the asset or mockup, your deadline, and any notes. It routes to your brand team at corporate, and you can track the status from the same page. Once it's decided, you'll see the outcome and any conditions attached.

## Brand and Assets

Brand is the standards reference. The actual files, your logos, photos, and templates, live in **Assets**, covered in its own article.
