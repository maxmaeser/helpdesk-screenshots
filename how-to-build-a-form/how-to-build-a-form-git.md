Forms let you collect structured information from applicants at different stages of their journey. You can add forms to portal steps or attach them to the signup page to gather data before an applicant even enters the portal.

### Creating a Form

Navigate to **Portals → Control** and select the **Forms** tab.

![Studio sidebar with Forms tab selected](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-nav-sidebar-2026-03-13.png)
Create a new form to open the form builder.

![Forms list with Actions dropdown](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-table-2026-03-13.png)
The builder has three panels:

- **Pages panel** (left): Manage and reorder your form's pages
- **Editor** (center): Build out each page with fields
- **Fields Library** (right): Browse, search, and create fields

![Form builder three-panel layout](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-builder-overview-2026-03-13.png)
### Adding Pages

Click the **+** button in the pages panel to add a new page. Each page has its own title and subtitle that you can edit directly in the center panel. Drag pages in the left panel to reorder them. To delete a page, open its menu and select **Delete Page**.

![Pages panel with add button](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-pages-panel-2026-03-13.png)
### Working with Fields

Fields live in the **Fields Library** on the right. To add a field to a page, drag it from the library and drop it onto the page editor. You can also reorder fields within a page by dragging them.

The library has three filter tabs: **All Fields**, **In Use** (currently placed on a page), and **Not In Use** (available but unassigned). Use the search bar to find fields by name.

![Fields library with filter tabs and search](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-fields-library-2026-03-13.png)
### Creating a New Field

Click **Create New Field** at the bottom of the fields library. Each field has two name properties:

- **Field Name**: An internal label for your team. This is how the field appears in the dashboard and library.
- **Field Question**: The text displayed to the applicant. Write this as a clear, answerable prompt.

![Create New Field dialog](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-create-field-2026-03-13.png)
You also configure the **Question Field Type** (text, single select, multi-select, dropdown, date, boolean, and others), set whether the field is **Required**, and optionally add a description and placeholder text.

![Question Field Type dropdown showing available types](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-field-types-2026-03-13.png)
For select-type fields, add at least two options. Drag options to reorder them.

### Field Categories

Assign a **Field Category** to control where the submitted data appears on your sales lead panels in the dashboard. You can create new categories directly from the field editor by clicking **Add Category** and entering a name.

![Field Category dropdown with category options](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-field-category-2026-03-13.png)
### Removing vs. Deleting a Field

There are two ways to take a field off a page:

- **Remove From Page**: Unlinks the field from the current page. The field stays in your library and can be placed again later.
- **Delete Field** (from the library menu): Permanently removes the field from your entire form. This cannot be undone.

![Field context menu showing Edit Field and Remove From Page](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/how-to-build-a-form/form-field-menu-2026-03-13.png)
### Using Forms in the Portal

Forms can appear in two places:

1. **As a portal step**: In the portal editor, add a step with the **Form** type and select the form you want to use.
2. **On the signup page**: In the signup builder under **Form Fields**, select a form to display additional fields when applicants first create their account.
