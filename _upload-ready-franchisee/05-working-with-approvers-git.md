Some project tasks in your portal have approvers attached. When you finish the work, the task moves into a pending state, and an approver signs off before it counts as done. The portal tracks who's on the hook for what, and tells you when you're waiting on someone.

## The five roles

Approvers are organized by a model called RACI-A.

- **Responsible**: the person who does the work. That's usually you on a franchisee task, and your name shows against the task as the one delivering it.
- **Accountable**: the person who owns the outcome and answers for the result. There's one Accountable per task.
- **Consulted**: anyone whose input is needed before the task can complete. The portal pings them for their feedback.
- **Informed**: anyone who needs to know what's going on with the task. They're notified on completion or rejection, with no action required.
- **Approver**: the final approval gate. The person or group who formally signs off before the task can be marked complete.

## How approval works

Each approver reviews the work on their own and approves or rejects it, with the option to leave feedback. The task shows as pending until they act.

## Tracking a pending task

When you finish a task that has approvers, you'll see an **Awaiting approval** heading on the task, with a note on how many approvers it's been sent to, and a **Pending** badge next to each approver's name and avatar. You can check the status of any pending task from your project view, or from Home.
