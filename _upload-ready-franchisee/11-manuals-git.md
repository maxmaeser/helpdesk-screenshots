> **[VIDEO EMBED: Module 11 — Manuals]**

Manuals is your operating reference: the brand's playbooks, SOPs, and standards. Learning is where you learn a new skill through sequenced courses. Manuals is where you look something up on the job. Two surfaces, two different jobs.

## Browsing and categories

Manuals opens on tabs across the top: **Browse**, **My List**, **Recommended**, **Recently Viewed**, and **Recently Updated**. Browse is the library itself, and any manuals you've bookmarked surface first there in a "Pick up where you left off" row.

![Manuals home showing tabs for Browse, My List, Recommended, and more, with category filter pills](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/11-manuals/manuals-home.png)
Below that sits a row of category pills, whatever categories your brand has set up. Pick one and the library filters down to it. There's also an **All categories** dropdown in the toolbar next to sort and the Grid/Cards view toggle, if you'd rather filter from there.

Bookmark a manual you reference often (the bookmark icon lives right on the manual) and it lands in **My List**, one tap away the next time you need it.

## Reading a manual

Each manual opens in a clean document view. A table of contents on the right lists the manual's main headings so you can jump to a section, and the body is laid out for reading on the floor or at a desk.

![Document view with a table of contents](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/11-manuals/manuals-document.png)
## Search

Search runs across the whole library, not only the category you're in. Type a phrase, and the results list every manual that matches, so you can open the right one straight from the search instead of hunting through categories.

![Search results for a phrase showing a result card with title and snippet](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/maxmaeser/helpdesk-screenshots/master/11-manuals/manuals-search.png)
## Good to know

Manuals is read only for franchisees. The source of truth is maintained by your brand, and when corporate updates a manual, the new version is what you see. Courses you complete in Learning often point back here, so think of Manuals as the long form reference behind the training.
