Most Project tasks in your portal have approvers attached. When you finish the work, the task moves into a pending state, and an approver signs off before it counts as done. The portal tracks who's on the hook for what, and tells you when you're waiting on someone.

## The five roles

Approvers are organized by a model called RACI-A.

- **Responsible**: the person who does the work. That's usually you on a franchisee task, and your name shows against the task as the one delivering it.
- **Accountable**: the person who approves the work and owns the outcome. There's one Accountable per task.
- **Consulted**: anyone whose input is needed before the task can complete. The portal pings them for their feedback.
- **Informed**: anyone who needs to know what's going on with the task. They're notified on completion or rejection, with no action required.
- **Approve**: the final approval gate. The person or group who formally signs off before the task can be marked complete.

## The three approval modes

The Approve role can work in three modes, depending on how the task is set up.

- **Sequential**: approvers go in a set order. The first approver signs off, then the second, until the chain is complete.
- **Anyone approves**: the first approver to sign off wins. Once one person approves, the task closes for the rest.
- **All approve**: each approver on the list has to sign off. The task stays pending until everyone has weighed in.

## Tracking a pending task

When you finish a task that has approvers, you'll see a yellow **Pending approval** badge on the card, with the approver's name and avatar next to it. You can check the status of any pending task from your project view, or from your home page activity feed.
